# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
The QA Lead owns the overall test strategy and quality gates for the project. They coordinate test execution across the team to ensure releases meet defined quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain the overall test strategy and test plans
- Coordinate test execution across developers and QA engineers
- Establish and enforce quality gates for release readiness
- Report test coverage, defect trends, and quality metrics
- Collaborate on acceptance criteria and definition of done

### Goals
- Ensure every release meets quality and reliability standards
- Reduce defect escape rate to production
- Build a culture of quality across the delivery team

### Typical Communication
- Works with Developers on test coverage requirements and shared testing responsibilities
- Coordinates with PM on release criteria and go/no-go decisions
- Aligns with Product Manager on acceptance criteria and expected user outcomes

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers design user flows, wireframes, and interactive prototypes to ensure the product is intuitive, accessible, and user-centric. They advocate for the end user throughout the product lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Create user flows, wireframes, mockups, and prototypes
- Conduct or synthesize user research to inform design decisions
- Define usability standards and interaction patterns
- Collaborate with engineering to ensure implementation feasibility
- Validate designs with stakeholders and gather feedback iteratively

### Goals
- Deliver experiences that are intuitive, accessible, and delightful
- Reduce usability issues found in QA or post-release
- Ensure user needs are represented throughout planning and delivery

### Typical Communication
- Works with Product Manager on requirements, user stories, and feature scope
- Collaborates with Developers on implementation feasibility and design handoffs
- Engages Stakeholders for design validation and user feedback sessions

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
The Release Manager plans and executes releases, ensures release readiness across all teams, and owns the deployment checklist and rollback plans. They act as the central coordinator during the release process.

### Responsibilities
- Plan release windows and coordinate release readiness across teams
- Maintain and execute the deployment checklist
- Own rollback and incident response plans for releases
- Coordinate communications around release events
- Track release metrics and post-release verification

### Goals
- Execute releases safely, predictably, and with minimal disruption
- Ensure all stakeholders are informed before, during, and after releases
- Continuously improve the release process based on retrospective findings

### Typical Communication
- Coordinates with PM on release timelines and scheduling
- Works with Developers to confirm readiness and resolve last-minute issues
- Partners with DevOps Engineer on pipeline readiness and deployment execution
- Notifies Stakeholders of upcoming releases, changes, and post-release status

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers maintain CI/CD pipelines, deployment infrastructure, and observability tooling. They support smooth releases and enable rapid incident response by ensuring the platform is stable and automated.

### Responsibilities
- Design, build, and maintain CI/CD pipelines and deployment automation
- Manage infrastructure, environments, and configuration management
- Implement and maintain observability tooling (monitoring, alerting, logging)
- Support incident response and post-incident remediation
- Enable developer productivity through reliable tooling and fast feedback loops

### Goals
- Keep deployment pipelines fast, reliable, and secure
- Minimize mean time to recovery (MTTR) for incidents
- Reduce toil through automation and infrastructure-as-code practices

### Typical Communication
- Works with Developers on pipeline integration, environment configuration, and tooling needs
- Coordinates with PM on deployment windows and infrastructure constraints
- Partners with Release Manager on release execution and rollback readiness

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers document product features, API references, release notes, and key project artifacts. They ensure that technical knowledge is accessible, accurate, and up to date for internal teams and end users.

### Responsibilities
- Author and maintain product documentation, user guides, and API references
- Draft release notes and changelogs for each release
- Document key project artifacts including runbooks, onboarding guides, and process docs
- Review documentation for accuracy in collaboration with developers and product teams
- Define documentation standards and templates for the team

### Goals
- Ensure all features and changes are clearly documented before release
- Reduce support burden by improving the quality of self-service documentation
- Keep documentation current and aligned with product evolution

### Typical Communication
- Coordinates with Product Manager on requirements, feature scope, and user personas
- Works with PM on documentation timelines aligned to the release schedule
- Collaborates with Developers to verify technical accuracy of documentation

---

## Agile Coach / Scrum Master

### Role Summary
The Agile Coach or Scrum Master facilitates agile ceremonies, removes team blockers, and supports continuous improvement. They help the team uphold agile values and practices to achieve sustainable velocity and team health.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate agile ceremonies: daily standups, sprint planning, sprint reviews, and retrospectives
- Identify and remove impediments that slow team progress
- Coach the team on agile principles and practices
- Track team health and velocity, and flag concerns to leadership
- Support the team in adopting process improvements identified in retrospectives

### Goals
- Enable the team to deliver consistently and sustainably
- Foster a culture of transparency, accountability, and continuous improvement
- Minimize blockers and unplanned disruptions to the team's flow

### Typical Communication
- Aligns with PM on process health, team ceremonies, and delivery cadence
- Works with Developers to understand velocity, blockers, and technical impediments
- Collaborates with Product Manager on backlog health, prioritization clarity, and planning readiness
